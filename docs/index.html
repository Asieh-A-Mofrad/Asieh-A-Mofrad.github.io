My research asks how learning, memory, and adaptive behaviour can be modelled in both natural and artificial systems. I combine formal modelling with applied behavioural data science, moving between graph-based associative memory, reinforcement learning, representation learning, and real-world longitudinal data.

I have developed this agenda through two complementary PhD trajectories in Informatics and Behavior Analysis, and I now work on projects spanning retail forecasting, prescription trajectories, digital behaviour, and computational models of cognition.

Research Profile

I develop computational models that connect learning mechanisms, structured representation, and real-world behavioural data. A consistent theme across my work is the search for models that are both powerful and interpretable: models that can capture complex adaptive behaviour while still offering theoretical insight.

Across theory and application, my work moves across three linked levels:

Formal models of memory and structure

Graph-based and brain-inspired associative memory models rooted in coding theory, sequence storage, and scalable retrieval.

Adaptive learning and reinforcement

Projective Simulation, EPS/E-EPS, contrastive reinforcement learning, and computational accounts of generalisation and stimulus equivalence.

Behavioural data in applied settings

Machine learning for retail, health registries, and digital traces, with emphasis on temporal structure, interpretability, and responsible AI.

Research Agenda

I am especially interested in building a cohesive programme around three themes: structured and neuro-symbolic intelligent systems, behavioural modelling in real-world contexts, and long-term feedback dynamics in adaptive digital environments. Across these themes, I aim to strengthen interpretability, robustness, and evaluation in behavioural data science and trustworthy AI.

Collaboration

I welcome collaborations across artificial intelligence, machine learning, behavioural science, cognitive modelling, recommender systems, and longitudinal data analysis. If your work touches learning, memory, structured representation, adaptive systems, or behaviour-rich data, there is a good chance we have something to talk about.
